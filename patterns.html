GREY — four unified logotypes.

Each proposal is one word, one mark — a complete logotype. All six channels (R G B C M Y) are present, but they do not fragment the word; they belong to it. Read as one thing, designed to be worn, stamped, printed, or embroidered.

GREY + spectrum punctum
The period

The word is set once, clean, in an editorial serif. A six-channel spectrum sits where a period would — six pigments compressed into a single punctuation mark. The word reads as one; the colour lives in the pause that follows.

GREY with underline spectrum
The underscore

A single mono-set GREY, sitting on a hair-thin six-channel baseline. The underline is not decoration — it is a line of descending pigments the word is anchored to. From a distance, one reads a word and a rule; up close, the rule is the entire visible spectrum.

The enclosure

Left bracket draws R / G / B — the three lights. Right bracket draws C / M / Y — the three pigments. The word GREY is held between them. The colours surround the word without entering it. A simple, containable mark, readable at any scale.

GREY with channel stamp
The signed word

A heavy mono word sits above a single line of channel letters — R G B · C M Y — each stamped in its own pigment. The rule and the hex caption below make the logotype legible as both a word and a technical signature. Looks equally at home on a tag, a book, or a sleeve.
