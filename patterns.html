6-channel offset / chromatic edge

Kirlian discharge, extended. A crisp GREY word at the centre, surrounded by the six channels offset around it — an electric corona that is both optical and philosophical. Variations differ in direction, intensity, blur, and typographic voice. Eight attempts at the same instinct.

The corona

Six copies offset in a hexagon around the word — R top, Y upper-right, G lower-right, C bottom, B lower-left, M upper-left. Each sits exactly 60° apart from the next. The sharp grey word stays readable; the six channels hum at its edges.

The glow

The six offset channels are blurred outward to an ethereal halo — Kirlian in its original spiritual-photographic sense. The grey letter stays razor sharp; the colour around it is atmosphere, not edge. Reads like radiation.

The whisper

Editorial serif with a 1–1.4px six-channel offset. You barely notice the chromatic edge until you come close; then it reveals as six pigments doing quiet work at the margins. Works across scales, especially small — labels, hem tags, print.

The left-right bleed

The six channels splay only on the x-axis — RGB pulled left of the word, CMY pulled right. Reads cleanly at a glance; inspects rich: it mirrors how film runs through a projector and how our eyes resolve the visible spectrum sequentially.

The individual

Each of the four letters carries a different pair of complementary ghosts. G holds R⁺C; R holds Y⁺B; E holds G⁺M; Y closes the loop. The logotype reads as one word, yet every letter has its own private observation.

The halo

The hex cardinal offset in serif, with a soft blur applied only to the colour layers. The grey word stays crystalline; its six coloured twins radiate behind it like a low-voltage halo. Editorial, calm, almost sacred.

The contour

The six channels appear only as hairline outlines — not filled letters. The result is a lighter, more architectural version of the Kirlian edge: less "energy field", more "technical drawing of energy field".

The two worlds

RGB above the word, CMY below. Light floats, pigment settles. The grey word is the horizon between them. Reads the same concept as 7-layer fold, but now as a single edge phenomenon, not a vertical slice.
